Charles Anthony Paris (born April 30, 1980), known professionally as Brother OTG, is an American record producer, composer, and music executive. His production work includes collaborations with artists such as 2 Chainz, Beanie Sigel, Scarface, and The Heatmakerz.[1] As a voting member of the Grammy Awards' Philadelphia and Chicago Chapters for over a decade, he has also contributed significantly to the music industry behind the scenes.[2]

Paris is also an educator in the producer community, creating tutorials and masterclasses for a variety of digital audio workstations (DAWs) and hardware, including the Akai MPC series and Roland SP-404 MKII.[3]

Early life and career

Paris was born in Urbana, Illinois. He developed an early passion for music, creating and selling mixtapes and later selling music for the local "Rap House." In the late 1990s, he moved to Decatur, Georgia, where he worked at the 285 Flea Market and began installing car stereos.[2] At age 19, he attended Atterbury Job Corps before formally beginning his career in music under the name "I Double L".

While building his music brand under his first label, Midwest Ill Muzik Group (ASCAP), he also trained and worked as a Journeyman Maintenance Mechanic. During his 30s, he relocated to Philadelphia, establishing himself as one of the notable producers in Philadelphia. There, he founded Over Time Grind Productions, LLC, and focused on the music business, managing artists and building brands. Concurrently, he had a successful career in automotive sales, selling over 2,000 cars in the tri-state area between 2015 and 2020.[1]

Currently, Paris focuses on teaching music production and serves as an advisor and consultant for brand building and music business strategy through his brand, SupplyTheWork.

Services

In his role as a consultant, Paris offers several services for creators and brands:

  • Music Licensing: Providing custom music for YouTube creators, filmmakers, and other media projects.
  • Brand Consulting: Advising artists and entrepreneurs on brand building and marketing strategy.
  • Producer Mentorship: One-on-one sessions for producers looking to advance their skills and business acumen.
